Getting Started
🚀 Quick start: Get a free API key at console.groq.com → paste it in Setup → pick a workspace → start chatting. That's it.
1. Choose a workspace from the home screen. Each workspace is a specialized AI tuned for a specific type of task — learning, coding, writing, fitness, business, and more. Pick the one that matches what you're trying to do.
2. Pick a mode from the sidebar. Within each workspace, modes change how the AI formats and approaches its response. For example in Resonant. Learn: "Lock In" gives fast direct answers, "Feynman" teaches by explanation, "Flashcard Gen" creates study cards.
3. Type your question and hit ↑ or Enter. The AI will respond with a typewriter effect — it streams in naturally. Each response has a 🔊 Speak button to read it aloud.
4. Use the shortcut chips above the input to add quick instructions like "bullet points only" or "summarise in 3 sentences" — these append to your message before sending.
Side Panels
Three panels live on the edges of the screen and are available in every workspace at all times.
📝 Notes
A full notepad with folders and auto-save. Open with the tab on the left/right edge, or press ' anywhere. Create folders, write freely, and your notes persist across sessions.
✅ Tasks
A simple to-do list always within reach. Open with the Tasks tab or press /. Check off tasks, delete them, and they persist across sessions.
📔 Journal
A private daily journal with AI chat inside it. Open with the Journal tab or press \. A new blank page opens each day. Use the History button to read past entries.
Drag tabs — click and drag any panel tab up or down to reposition it, or drag it to the other side of the screen. Opening Notes when Tasks is already open will automatically animate Tasks to the opposite side.
⇄ Panels Side button in the input toolbar toggles all panels between left and right side instantly.
Voice & Display
🔊 Text-to-Speech — Every AI response has a 🔊 Speak button. A Voice / Speed bar appears above the input in every workspace. Choose a voice, set speed (0.1× for slow dictation pace to 2.5× for fast), and adjust pitch.
★ High Quality Voices (ElevenLabs) — The top voices in the dropdown (Sarah, Liam, Lily, Daniel, Charlotte, Callum) use ElevenLabs for studio-quality audio. The first time you click Speak with a ★ voice, you'll be asked for your free ElevenLabs API key. Free tier gives plenty of monthly characters. If you skip it, the best available browser voice is used instead.
Text size — Use the − / + buttons next to "Text" in the input toolbar to adjust chat text size. This persists across sessions.
Dark / Light mode — Click ☀ / 🌙 in any topbar. The particle background adapts: white/grey dots on the landing page, colored dots matching each workspace's theme.
Tagging Messages
Tag any message by clicking the 🏷 Tag button below it. Pick one or more tags from the modal. Tagged messages appear with coloured labels below them.
Remove a tag by clicking the small ✕ on the tag label itself.
Search tags using the 🏷 button in the topbar of Resonant. Build — this opens the tag search panel where you can filter all tagged messages and jump to any of them.

Dock & Navigation
Bottom dock — visible only on the landing page. Contains: theme toggle ☀/🌙, ⇄ Panels side switch, ⚙ API Key, Guide, Bug Report, ⊹ Layout mode, Hub, and My Workspace.
Collapse dock — click the ‹ button on the left of the dock, or press = to collapse the dock to a small circle. Press again to expand.
⊹ Layout mode — switches the landing page from card grid to an interactive orbital view. Each workspace is a planet orbiting the Resonant.AI black hole center. Hover a planet for 0.5s to see its description slide in from the right. Click a planet or the Launch button to navigate. Entering/exiting has a morph animation.
Workspace number shortcuts — on the landing page, press 1–9 and 0 to instantly jump to any workspace. The number is shown in the top-right of each card.
Side Panels
Three panels — Notes (\ key), Journal (' key), Tasks (/ key). Each has its own tab on the side of the screen that you can click to open or close.
Independent sides — each panel can live on left or right independently. The ⇄ button in the dock moves all three at once. The backtick key (when not in an input) also toggles all panels.
Opening a second panel — if Notes is open and you open Tasks, the Tasks tab automatically animates to the opposite side so both panels can be open simultaneously.
Closing a crossed panel — when a panel that was moved to the opposite side closes, its tab animates back to its home side.
📁 Notes folders — create custom folders with the + Folder button. Hover a custom folder to see ✏️ rename and ✕ delete buttons. Folders persist across sessions.
📔 Journal — private daily journal with AI chat inside it. A new blank page opens each day. History button shows past entries.
Image Uploads & Vision
🖼️ Attach images — every workspace and the Build chat has an image attach button next to the input. Click it to select one or more images, or drag and drop images directly onto any chat area. Images appear as thumbnails above the input before sending.
Vision AI — when you send a message with an image, the AI uses a vision model to analyse it. Ask about diagrams, screenshots, sketches, photos — anything visual. Images are not stored between messages.
Lightbox — click any image thumbnail to view it full-screen. Press Escape or click outside to close.
Found a Bug?
If something isn't working right, we'd love to know. 🐛 Submit a bug report →
You can also find the bug report button in the dock at the bottom of the home screen, in the top corner of this guide, and as a small 🐛 icon in the bottom-right corner on every screen.
Tone & Voice
🎭 Personality / Tone picker — Every workspace has a tone picker in its topbar. Choose from: 😎 Casual (smart friend), 👔 Formal (professional), ⚡ Blunt (no fluff, direct), 🌟 Encouraging (warm and motivating), 🎓 Tutor (explains step by step, checks understanding). The tone affects how the AI writes its responses, not what it knows.
📖 Explain toggle — When on, the AI shows its reasoning. When off, it gives the direct answer only. Explain is most useful in Learn and Research modes. Turn it off when you just need the output fast.
Workspaces

Resonant. Build
Hardware Engineering
▶
Your primary engineering workspace. Specialized in mechanical design, material science, structural analysis, manufacturing processes and physical prototyping. The AI knows real material grades, tolerances and manufacturing constraints. Uses your project history across all 5 modes.
💬 General
Your open-ended engineering advisor. Ask anything across mechanical design, CAD, materials or manufacturing. The AI connects topics across disciplines — ask about a material and it will also flag manufacturing implications.
🔬 Research
Strict precision mode. Every specification and number is treated seriously — the AI flags uncertain data with ⚠️ and tells you exactly how to verify it (standard number, datasheet, test method). Use this before committing to a design decision.
🔍 Critique
Structured design review. Returns a formatted response every time: Strengths → Concerns ranked by severity → Specific recommendations → Comparison to industry benchmarks. Paste a description or screenshot of your design for a full review.
💡 Brainstorm
Generates numbered, bold-titled engineering ideas with advantages, challenges and a feasibility rating per idea. Produces at least 5 concepts before narrowing. Ends with "Most promising:" based on your constraints. Good for early design exploration.
🔧 Problem Solver
Systematic fault diagnosis. Always returns: Root Cause → Failure Mechanism → 3 Fix Options ranked quick-to-robust → Verification method. If your description is vague, it will ask one targeted question before diagnosing.
🎓
Resonant. Learn
Accelerated Learning
▶
Designed for focused studying, exam prep and rapid understanding. Has a personality toggle (Casual/Formal/Blunt/Encouraging/Tutor) and an accent color picker in the sidebar. The Explain toggle is especially useful here — turn it on to understand concepts, turn it off when you just need the answer.
⚡ Lock In
Maximum focus mode. Leads immediately with the direct answer — no preamble, no restating the question. Short, scannable, bulleted. Best when you need answers fast and already understand the context.
🧠 Feynman Technique
Explains concepts as if teaching a non-expert — plain language, everyday analogies and no unexplained jargon. Based on Richard Feynman's learning principle: if you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet. Best for genuinely confusing concepts.
🃏 Flashcard Gen
Converts any topic into Q&A study flashcards, ordered from foundational to advanced. Generates 8-15 cards minimum. With Explain on, adds a "Why it matters" note to each card. Ends with challenge cards testing application not just recall. Good before exams.
🔬 Deep Research
Thorough, structured analysis. Uses headers, covers multiple perspectives and cites reasoning. With Explain on, shows the analytical methodology. Ends with "Further reading" suggestions. Use this when you need to genuinely understand a topic not just get an answer.
🔩
Resonant. Forge
Software Engineering
▶
Senior-level software engineering workspace. Always produces complete, production-quality code — not pseudocode or stubs. Thinks about edge cases, error handling and maintainability without being asked. Paste code directly into the input — it reads it and responds appropriately.
🐛 Debugger
Systematic bug diagnosis. Returns: Root Cause → Why It Breaks → Complete corrected code → Verification method. Never just says "try this" — always explains the exact failure mechanism first.
🏗️ Code Architect
Design patterns, file structure and code organization. Rates existing code on readability, testability and scalability before suggesting improvements. Pushes back on over-engineering when a simpler approach exists.
🔧 System Design
Database, API, service and infrastructure architecture. Uses ASCII diagrams. Always addresses scalability, failure modes and data consistency. Recommends the simplest architecture that meets requirements.
📄 Documentation
README files, inline comments, JSDoc/docstring annotations, changelogs and API reference docs. Writes comments that explain WHY not WHAT. Matches formality to context (open-source vs internal team).
🔌 API Integration
Endpoint design, authentication, rate limiting, versioning and third-party integration code. Provides complete working examples with error handling — not just happy-path code. Always addresses status codes and structured error responses.
📐
Resonant. Script
CAD Scripting
▶
Generative geometry and CAD scripting workspace. Produces complete, runnable scripts — never skeleton code. Understands geometric fundamentals deeply including coordinate systems, transformation matrices and parametric constraint logic. The input has a monospace font to make it easier to paste code.
🔮 Auto-Fusion
Generates complete Fusion 360 Python API scripts using adsk.fusion and adsk.core imports. Includes error handling, a run() entry point, parameter variables at the top and instructions for running the script in Fusion 360 (Tools > Scripts and Add-Ins).
📦 Live-SCAD
Generates parametric OpenSCAD code. All dimensions are named variables at the top. Uses modules for reusable geometry. Notes where support material would be needed for 3D printing and suggests modifications to reduce overhangs.
📐 Geo-Math
Precise geometric calculations with full working shown. Angles, dimensions, compound cuts, geometric relationships and stress calculations. Results to 0.1mm and 0.1° precision. Always shows the calculation steps so you can verify and adapt them.
🗂️ STEP-Insight
Analysis of STEP file data, geometry descriptions or CAD model structure. Identifies geometric primitives, assembly relationships and potential design issues. Looks for manufacturability problems, stress concentrations and tolerance stack-up issues in assemblies.

Resonant. Create
Content & Creative
▶
Content creation and creative strategy workspace. Actively avoids generic AI writing — no "delve into", "game-changer" or excessive hedging. Has strong creative opinions and will tell you directly when something is not working and why. Good for brand identity, social content, video scripts and layout direction.
✍️ Writing
Essays, social posts, emails, captions and any text content. Matches the platform and tone exactly. Offers a variation in a different style if the first draft might not land. Actively avoids AI-sounding language.
👁 Visual Logic
Design feedback focused on visual hierarchy, spacing, contrast and typography. Gives specific values ("increase padding to 48px") not vague direction ("add more space"). References real design principles by name.
✨ Branding
Brand identity development: name rationale, 3 core values, specific target audience description, tone of voice with examples and what the brand is NOT. Pushes hard against generic positioning.
🎬 Video Scripts
Platform-specific scripts (YouTube, TikTok, Reels, Podcast). Writes the hook first and confirms direction before the full script. Includes pacing cues. YouTube hooks in 15 seconds, TikTok in 3.
🗂️ Layout Design
Layout and design system guidance with real specificity — 8pt grid, typographic scale, WCAG contrast requirements. Gives specific pixel/rem values for spacing, font sizes and line heights.

Resonant. Pulse
Performance
▶
Performance science workspace for athletes across all sports. Evidence-based and specific — gives real numbers (sets, reps, percentages, gram amounts per kg) not vague directional advice. Always asks about your sport, level and goals when that context is needed. Flags when professional guidance is recommended.
🏋️ Training
Periodized training programs for any sport. Gives specific session structures (e.g. "4x4 min at 90-95% max HR, 3 min recovery") using RPE, HR zones or power zones. Addresses periodization phases and flags overtraining warning signs.
🥗 Nutrition
Sport nutrition with specific numbers — gram amounts per kg bodyweight, timing relative to training, hydration strategy with electrolyte guidance. Only recommends evidence-backed supplements (creatine, caffeine, beta-alanine). Distinguishes training day vs rest day needs.
🏁 Competition
Full competition preparation: taper protocol, pre-event nutrition and sleep, warm-up structure, race execution pacing models and mental preparation techniques. Includes contingency plans for when things go wrong mid-race.
🛌 Recovery
Evidence-based recovery starting with sleep (the most powerful tool). Then nutrition timing, active recovery, cold/heat therapy with honest evidence assessment. Helps identify cumulative overreaching before it becomes injury.
🦴 Biomechanics
Movement pattern analysis, compensation identification and corrective exercises with sets, reps and coaching cues. Explains the kinetic chain — how a restriction in one area affects movement elsewhere. Always recommends professional physiotherapy for pain-related issues.

Resonant. Lens
Market Intel
▶
Market intelligence and strategic business workspace. Combines strategy consulting rigour with venture analyst pattern recognition. Does not hedge conclusions into uselessness — when evidence points somewhere, it says so clearly. Distinguishes between what it knows, what it is estimating and what is genuinely uncertain.
📈 Trend Forecasting
Identifies structural shifts vs hype cycles. For each trend: evidence strength, timeline, primary drivers, who benefits, who is disrupted and the second-order effects most people miss. References specific companies and data as evidence. Flags over-crowded spaces vs genuine white space.
🗺️ Competitor Mapping
Maps direct, indirect and non-obvious potential competitors. For each: positioning, pricing model, strengths, weaknesses and recent strategic moves. Identifies the key competitive battlegrounds and where there is genuinely uncontested space.
🎤 Pitch Deck
Investor-ready narrative structure. Addresses the investor's unspoken questions. Uses bottom-up market sizing (not "1% of $100B"). Removes buzzwords and replaces with specific descriptions. Honest about risks — sophisticated investors respect founders who know their weak spots.
💰 Financial Modeling
Builds honest financial models with all assumptions labelled and confidence levels noted. Includes unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback), monthly cash flow for 18-24 months and three scenarios (conservative/base/optimistic). Identifies the 2-3 assumptions that most affect the outcome.

FAQ
What AI model powers Resonant AI? ▾
All workspaces use Llama 3.3 70B via Groq's free API — one of the most capable open-source models available. Groq's custom inference hardware (LPUs) makes it dramatically faster than running the same model on standard GPU infrastructure. You get near-instant responses for a 70 billion parameter model that matches GPT-4 level capability on many tasks.
Is my data saved anywhere? ▾
Your API key, chat history, notes and tasks are stored only in your browser's localStorage — they never leave your device except as part of the API call to Groq to generate a response. Groq processes your messages to generate replies but does not train on them by default. If you want zero local history, use Incognito mode in any workspace — it clears history after each session.
Why does the AI sometimes get things wrong? ▾
Large language models can hallucinate — confidently state incorrect information. This is why Resonant. Build's Research mode is specifically designed to flag uncertain data with ⚠️ and tell you how to verify it. For any critical engineering, medical or financial decision, always verify numbers and specifications from primary sources (manufacturer datasheets, engineering standards, professional advice). Never trust any AI output for safety-critical decisions without independent verification.
How do Notes and Tasks work across workspaces? ▾
Notes (left edge tab) and Tasks (right edge tab) are global — they are accessible from every workspace and screen. Notes have folder organization and auto-save as you type. Tasks are a simple checklist that persists across sessions. The Project Manager mode in Resonant. Learn reads your task list automatically as context so it can help you plan around what you're already working on.
What is the Workspace and the Hub? ▾
The Hub (⊞) is the full model library — browse every workspace and mode, read what each does and pin the ones you use most. The Workspace (⌂) is your personal dashboard — pinned modes appear as quick-launch cards, recent activity is logged, and your notes and tasks are previewed. Pin frequently used modes in the Hub to build a Workspace that reflects how you actually work.
How do I delete messages? ▾
In Resonant. Build (the main Design mode), use the ☑ select button in the topbar to enter select mode — check individual messages and hit Delete to remove them. In all other workspaces, hover over any message bubble to reveal the ✕ button for single-message deletion, or use the ☑ select button in the topbar for bulk deletion. Deleted messages cannot be recovered.
